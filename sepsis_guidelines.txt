Sepsis-3 Definitions and Clinical Criteria

Sepsis is defined as life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection.

1. Organ Dysfunction
   - Represented by an increase in the Sequential Organ Failure Assessment (SOFA) score of 2 points or more.
   - For patients with infections, an increase of 2 SOFA points is associated with an in-hospital mortality greater than 10%.

2. Clinical Criteria for Screening (qSOFA)
   - Altered mental status (Glasgow Coma Scale < 15)
   - Fast respiratory rate (>= 22 breaths/min)
   - Low blood pressure (systolic blood pressure <= 100 mmHg)

3. Septic Shock
   - A subset of sepsis in which underlying circulatory and cellular/metabolic abnormalities are profound enough to substantially increase mortality.
   - Clinical criteria:
     - Persisting hypotension requiring vasopressors to maintain MAP >= 65 mmHg
     - Serum lactate level > 2 mmol/L (18 mg/dL) despite adequate volume resuscitation.

4. The Sepsis Bundle (Hour-1 Bundle)
   - Measure lactate level. Remeasure if initial lactate is > 2 mmol/L.
   - Obtain blood cultures before administering antibiotics.
   - Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics.
   - Begin rapid administration of 30mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate >= 4 mmol/L.
   - Apply vasopressors if hypotensive during or after fluid resuscitation to maintain MAP >= 65 mmHg.
